Joel Blenman | Aspiring Software Engineer

Not too long ago, there was a time when I aspired to become an architect. My passion lay in building technology and technical drawing during my school days because I had a genuine love for design and creativity. However, the construction aspect of architecture didn't hold much appeal for me. It wasn't until later that I realized my knack for software and my ability to effectively use various software tools (although I never truly mastered any of them). My software choices primarily revolved around architecture and graphic design, including programs like Blender, AutoCAD, and SketchUp. Interestingly, what I didn't foresee was that I would eventually pursue computer science at UWI (University of the West Indies). Prior to that, I had never studied IT or computer science in high school or college. But once I started learning, I couldn't quench my thirst for knowledge. I yearned to understand everything, from design patterns and object-oriented programming to software engineering, algorithm design, UI/UX, and mobile development. The world of computer science offered an endless realm of possibilities and learning opportunities, which is exactly what I had craved back when I contemplated a career in architecture.
